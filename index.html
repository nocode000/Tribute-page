Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

The man who fights for civil rights and freedom across the world.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1869 - Born in Gujarat.
  • 1883 - He moved to South Africa to represent an Indian merchant in a lawsuit.
  • 1915 - He returned to India. He set about organising peasants, farmers, and urban labourers to protest against excessive land-tax and discrimination.
  • 1921 - Gandhi adopted the use of an Indian loincloth (short dhoti) and a shawl (in the winter) woven with yarn hand-spun on a traditional Indian spinning wheel (charkha) as a sign of identification with India's rural poor.
  • 1930 - Bringing anti-colonial nationalism to the common Indians, Gandhi led them in challenging the British-imposed salt tax with the 400 km (250 mi) Dandi Salt March in 1930 and in calling for the British to quit India in 1942.
  • 1937 - The University of Nagpur awarded him an LL.D.
  • 1940 - Gandhi's vision of an independent India based on religious pluralism was challenged in the early 1940s by a new Muslim nationalism which demanded a separate Muslim homeland carved out of India.
  • 1947 - Britain granted independence, but the British Indian Empire was partitioned into two dominions, the Hindu-majority India and the Muslim-majority Pakistan.
  • 1948 - Dies at the age of 78
